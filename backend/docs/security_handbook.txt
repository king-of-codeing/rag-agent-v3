ACME CORPORATION — INFORMATION SECURITY HANDBOOK
Version 4.0

SECTION 1: ACCEPTABLE USE
Page 1
Acme systems and data may only be used for authorized business purposes.
Personal use of company devices is permitted only during breaks and must not interfere with work.
Streaming, gaming, and torrenting on the corporate network are strictly prohibited.

SECTION 2: PHISHING AND EMAIL SECURITY
Page 2
Never click links in unsolicited emails, even from familiar-looking senders.
Verify suspicious emails by contacting the sender through a separate channel.
Report all phishing attempts to security@acme-example.com immediately.
Phishing simulations are conducted quarterly; failure requires mandatory retraining.

SECTION 3: DEVICE SECURITY
Page 3
All company laptops must have full-disk encryption enabled (FileVault for Mac, BitLocker for Windows).
Screen lock must engage after 5 minutes of inactivity.
Lost or stolen devices must be reported within 1 hour to IT security.
Two-factor authentication is mandatory for all corporate accounts.

SECTION 4: DATA CLASSIFICATION
Page 4
Public: marketing materials, public press releases.
Internal: org charts, internal wikis, project plans.
Confidential: customer data, financial records, source code.
Restricted: trade secrets, M&A discussions, security keys.
Confidential and Restricted data must never leave approved systems.

SECTION 5: INCIDENT REPORTING
Page 5
Report any suspected security incident to security@acme-example.com within 1 hour.
Do not attempt to investigate or remediate yourself.
Preserve evidence — do not delete logs, emails, or files related to the incident.
On-call security responds 24/7 at extension SEC-911.
